  • Autological words

    Many times in the past I have heard some word and noticed that it could, in some way, be said to describe itself. I never really had a precise way of describing or putting a name to this phenomenon until today when I looked up "words that describe themselves" in google and came across what I was looking for on the wikipedia page for the Grellingâ€“Nelson paradox which mentioned autological words.

    For me, when I would think about this over the last couple years the perenial example I would always go back to was synonym. I would say synonym was a synonym of synonym so it describes itself, although actually a synonym of a word has to be a different word so that doesn't really make sense. But then I would also think of antonym which is a real autological word which describes itself because of course it is the antonym of the word synonym.

    I found a big list of autological words by Henry Segerman but I believe there are some I've thought of in the past which are not there. I should create a list of them somewhere sometime. He talks about the Grelling-Nelson paradox there as well. Basically its about whether heterological (meaning a word that doesn't describe itself) describes itself which results in a paradox.

    Which is good! Because as we all know, paradoxes are contradictions, and contraditions are fun!

  • Class Registration Tips

    1. Complete the pre-registration steps, (you won't want to be caught at the last minute having to rush through these)
    2. When you want to add a class to myplan, always check the enrollment restrictions by clicking on the enrollment restrictions tag or view more details, classes will often have requirements based on major or class standing and if you don't meet those requirements your entire registration will be denied, potentialy losing you out on a spot in another class and you'll have to remove this class anyway.
    3. Make sure you know what day you are registering for and that you set an alarm for something like fifteen minutes before 6am, you don't want to let some unforseen problem or slow boot up to make you miss registration if you wake up too close to 6am.
    4. As 6am approaches hover your mouse over the send to registration button and click it 1 to 2 seconds before 6 am actually arrives. Set up a clock with the time up to the second so you can see this. Even though you are clicking send before 6am it will still accept it in my experience, and classes are first come first served so getting in even a moment earlier might make the difference for you.
    5. Be ready to register with sln as a backup. When the traffic is really high with everyone trying to register all at the same moment, sometimes the whole site has been brought to a standstill with people unable to register while stuck on frozen or loading pages. Registering with sln can sometimes get around this slowdown and save you from the UW's apparently awful prepardness for the flood of 6am registrations that they mandated.
